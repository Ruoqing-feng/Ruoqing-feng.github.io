Ruoqing Feng

I am a computational biologist and PhD student at the German Center for Neurodegenerative Diseases (DZNE; LMU-GSN), working with Prof. Mikael Simons. I study how neuroinflammatory programs are organized in space and time within the central nervous system, leveraging single-cell and spatial transcriptomics to resolve cell states, their interactions, and the tissue microenvironments that sustain pathology. My goal is to bridge molecular profiles with histological context, identifying disease-relevant cellular niches and actionable pathways in chronic active multiple sclerosis lesions. I also enjoy building reproducible analysis pipelines and quantitative frameworks that help make complex spatial data interpretable and biologically testable.
